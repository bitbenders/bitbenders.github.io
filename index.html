Problem Space

According to the USDA, the average cost to raise a child up to the age of 17 is $233,610 [16]. Raising a child can be expensive. We want to design a better way for mothers of babies and toddlers to lower their living costs through a shared community of other mothers for this project. Oftentimes motherhood communities are centered around children and how their needs can be fulfilled, and thus catering to the needs of the mother is oftentimes neglected [17]. It is crucial that we understand and explore the needs of mothers with young children without only focusing on their children. Making sure the needs of a mother are met can ensure that a child’s needs can efficiently be met as well.

Key Findings

We sent out surveys to our target users, conducted semi-structured interviews with the said users and did affinity mapping as a group. The major research findings are

  1. A mother’s life - including daily routines, personal changes, and social life is greatly influenced by their children.
  2. Mothers believe that their child’s safety factors into everything they do.
  3. Mothers of babies and toddlers wishes that they had more alone time to relax and have fun.
  4. Mothers believe that support groups help them with their personal and mom life.
  5. Mothers feel that support groups can be overwhelming.
  6. Mothers are more likely to give clothes away to people they know before selling them to strangers.
  7. Mothers will not give away their child’s sentimental items.
  8. Mothers find second-hand shopping great for items that have great quality at a low cost.
  9. Mothers find that sustainability mainly matters financially.
  10. Mothers find cost and quality equally important while shopping.
  11. Mothers shopping methods depends on ease and timeliness
  12. Mothers find that their shopping experience is great when they as well as their children are satisfied.

Design Requirements

Based on our user research and analysis, we extracted a set of functional and non functional design requirements for our final solution.

Our functional requirements are :

  1. Make secondhand shopping quick and easy.
  2. Connect moms to their support groups.
  3. Help moms plan and manage their time and activities.
  4. Our design should offer suggestions for sustainable choices in the right context.
  5. Our design should provide the information the mom needs at every stage of the child growth.

Our non functional requirements are :

  1. Easy and simple to learn and use
  2. Stress-free and inclusive
  3. Safety and privacy options for how much moms choose to share of their profiles
  4. Promote and encourage sustainability
  5. Should foster a sense of community
